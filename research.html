Research

I am a PhD Candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at University of Toronto. I develop physics-based and data-driven models for electrochemical energy systems, with a focus on electrode-electrolyte interfaces in batteries and energy storage devices.


What I work on

  • Battery interfaces: ion adsorption, solvent polarization, and nanoscale charge storage at electrified surfaces.
  • Multiscale modeling: linking atomistic simulations to continuum transport (PDE) descriptions.
  • Electrochemical diagnostics: extracting actionable information from impedance and time-series data.

Research skills

  • Atomistic simulation workflows: molecular dynamics (MD) and Monte Carlo (MC) for confined interfaces and adsorption.
  • Continuum modeling: PDE-based transport and reduced-order models for device-scale predictions.
  • Model development: building constitutive relations and interface laws from simulation/experimental data.
  • Reproducible research: Python-based analysis pipelines, validation, and clean data management.

Data analytics & machine learning (advanced)

I use data analytics and machine learning to turn large datasets (simulations and experiments) into predictive models.

  • Scientific data processing: cleaning, feature engineering, uncertainty-aware analysis, and visualization.
  • Supervised learning: regression/classification for diagnostics, property prediction, and degradation trends.
  • Time-series modeling: extracting health indicators and operating-condition signatures from EIS/operando data.
  • Model selection and evaluation: cross-validation, error analysis, and interpretability-focused workflows.

Self-driving labs (SDLs) & autonomous discovery

I am interested in self-driving lab workflows that close the loop between experiments/simulations, data acquisition, and automated decision-making.

  • Closed-loop optimization: Bayesian/active learning strategies to select the next experiment or simulation.
  • Automation-ready pipelines: standardized data formats, APIs, and reproducible execution for high-throughput studies.
  • Digital twins: coupling mechanistic electrochemistry models with data-driven surrogates for fast control and design.

What I can help with

  • Building multiscale models that connect molecular interface physics to continuum transport and device response.
  • Designing MD/MC simulation campaigns and extracting publishable physical insights (structure–property relations).
  • Developing ML models for electrochemical diagnostics (e.g., impedance-driven performance/degradation prediction).
  • Creating end-to-end data workflows: from raw data → features → models → validated predictions.

Methods & tools

  • Molecular simulation: MD, MC; electrified interfaces; adsorption and equilibrium sampling.
  • Continuum modeling: transport equations and PDE-based frameworks.
  • Programming: Python (data analytics, automation, modeling), C (numerical workflows as needed).
  • HPC: MPI-based parallel runs; cluster workflows; high-throughput studies.
